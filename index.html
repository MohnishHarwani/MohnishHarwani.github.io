About Me

I am an undergraduate student at Purdue University majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics (GPA: 3.89). My research focuses on developing robust learning algorithms, meta-learned optimizers, and scalable model architectures that enable deep neural networks to train more efficiently and generalize better.

I work at the intersection of optimization, meta-learning, and foundation models, with applications in LLMs, vision-language models, and scientific ML.

I am currently seeking PhD positions for Fall 2026.

Research Themes

My work is motivated by instability in modern deep learning optimization pipelines and the need for scalable, self-improving learning algorithms. I’m especially interested in optimizers for large-scale models, LLM reasoning dynamics, and the interplay between architecture and optimization.

Projects

  • nanoHUB AURA — Large-scale LLM-powered scientific assistant system; released as a ChemRxiv preprint. Uses multi-agent architectures and dynamically growing datasets to support physics, materials science, and simulation workflows.
  • Zeroeth-Order Neural Optimizer — Implemented CMA-ES–based training algorithm achieving faster initial optimization than Adam/SGD; demonstrated effectiveness for non-smooth neural loss landscapes.
  • Robust Learned Optimizers with SAM — Integrated SAM into meta-optimizer training to address optimizer collapse and improve generalization across datasets and model architectures.
  • KPEvents (Kaiser Permanente) — On-device AI-powered health calendar built with Apple Foundation Models, generating personalized fitness and medication routines while preserving user privacy.
